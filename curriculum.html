Curriculum Guide

Explore the specific learning objectives, core subjects, and special projects for each grade level at Hopespring.

K

Kindergarten: Foundation

Our Kindergarten program focuses on social-emotional growth and the building blocks of literacy and numeracy. We create a nurturing environment where children learn to ask questions and work together.

menu_book Core Subjects

  • • Reading: Letter recognition, phonics, and early sight words.
  • • Math: Counting to 100, basic shapes, interaction with patterns.
  • • Science: Observation of seasons, plants, and animals.
  • • Social Studies: Understanding community helpers and family structures.

palette Special Projects

  • • "All About Me" Book: Students create a portfolio about their families and interests.
  • • Community Garden: Planting and caring for a class garden plot.
  • • 100 Days of School: A celebration of counting and milestones.
1

First Grade: Discovery

First Grade is a year of rapid growth in reading fluency and mathematical reasoning. Students begin to explore the wider world and their place within it through inquiry-based projects.

menu_book Core Subjects

  • • Reading: Decoding complex words, reading comprehension, and storytelling.
  • • Math: Addition and subtraction within 20, place value basics.
  • • Science: Weather patterns, states of matter (solids, liquids).
  • • Social Studies: Maps, globes, and basic geography.

science Special Projects

  • • Weather Station: Daily tracking and reporting of local weather.
  • • Animal Habitat Dioramas: Creating model habitats for chosen animals.
  • • Author's Tea: Sharing original stories with parents and peers.
2

Second Grade: Exploration

Second graders transition from learning to read to reading to learn. They tackle multi-step problems and engage in deeper scientific investigation and historical exploration.

menu_book Core Subjects

  • • Reading: Chapter books, fluency, and main idea identification.
  • • Math: Addition/Subtraction within 100, introduction to money and time.
  • • Science: Life cycles, forces and motion.
  • • Social Studies: Ancestry, culture, and traditions around the world.

public Special Projects

  • • Family Tree Project: exploring personal heritage and history.
  • • Simple Machines: Building functional levers, pulleys, and ramps.
  • • Cultural Fair: Presenting food, dress, and facts from a chosen country.
3

Third Grade: Expansion

Third grade marks a shift towards independence. Students master multiplication, dive into chapter book series, and begin to understand complex systems in science and society.

menu_book Core Subjects

  • • Reading: Determining theme, point of view, and text evidence.
  • • Math: Multiplication and division, introduction to fractions.
  • • Science: Ecosystems, adaptations, and weather hazards.
  • • Social Studies: Local government, economics, and civics.

rocket_launch Special Projects

  • • Market Day: Students create products and manage a mock classroom economy.
  • • Biome Research: Creating detailed reports and models of world biomes.
  • • Local History Tour: Visiting landmarks and documenting local history.
4

Fourth Grade: Analysis

Fourth graders become critical thinkers. They analyze texts deeply, solve multi-step word problems, and conduct scientific experiments with variables and hypotheses.

menu_book Core Subjects

  • • Reading: Comparing genres, analyzing text structure, and research skills.
  • • Math: Multi-digit arithmetic, fraction equivalence, and geometry.
  • • Science: Energy transfer, waves, and earth systems.
  • • Social Studies: State history, geography, and resources.

lightbulb Special Projects

  • • State Float Parade: Creating floats representing the history of a state.
  • • Energy Audit: Analyzing school energy use and proposing savings.
  • • Invention Convention: Designing and prototyping a solution to a problem.
5

Fifth Grade: Leadership

As the leaders of the elementary school, fifth graders refine their academic skills and prepare for middle school. They engage in complex debates, long-term projects, and leadership roles.

menu_book Core Subjects

  • • Reading: Novel studies, literary analysis, and persuasive writing.
  • • Math: Decimals, volume, and operations with larger fractions.
  • • Science: Structure of matter, ecosystems, and space systems.
  • • Social Studies: Early American history and constitutional foundations.

diversity_2 Special Projects

  • • Mock Trial: Simulating a court case to understand the justice system.
  • • Science Fair: Conducting and presenting an independent experiment.
  • • Legacy Project: A class service project to leave a mark on the school.
